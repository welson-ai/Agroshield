# Frequently Asked Questions

*Last updated: April 23, 2026*

## General Questions

### What is AgroShield?
AgroShield is a decentralized parametric crop insurance protocol built on the Celo blockchain. It provides automated insurance payouts based on weather conditions, eliminating the need for manual claims processing.

### How does parametric insurance work?
Parametric insurance automatically triggers payouts when predefined weather conditions (like rainfall levels) are met, without requiring manual assessment or proof of loss.

### What blockchain is AgroShield built on?
AgroShield is built on the Celo blockchain, chosen for its mobile-first approach, low transaction costs, and stable coin (cUSD) support.

## Insurance Policies

### What crops are covered?
We support major agricultural crops including:
- Grains: Wheat, corn, rice, barley
- Legumes: Soybeans, lentils, chickpeas
- Cash crops: Cotton, coffee, sugar cane
- Specialty crops: Fruits, vegetables, nuts

### How are premiums calculated?
Premiums are based on:
- **Coverage Amount**: Higher coverage = higher premium
- **Crop Type**: Riskier crops have higher premiums
- **Location**: Some regions have higher weather risk
- **Duration**: Longer policies have higher total premiums
- **Rainfall Threshold**: Lower thresholds = higher premiums

### What is a rainfall threshold?
This is the minimum amount of rainfall required to avoid triggering a payout. If actual rainfall falls below this threshold, an automatic payout is triggered.

### Can I have multiple policies?
Yes! You can create multiple policies for different crops, locations, or time periods to diversify your coverage.

## Claims and Payouts

### How do I file a claim?
You don't need to file a claim! With parametric insurance, payouts are automatically triggered when weather conditions meet the policy criteria.

### How long do payouts take?
Payouts are typically processed within minutes of the weather oracle confirming that conditions have been met.

### What if the weather data is wrong?
We use multiple weather data sources and require consensus before triggering payouts, reducing the risk of incorrect data.

### Can I appeal a payout decision?
Since payouts are automated based on objective weather data, there's no appeal process. This ensures fairness and transparency.

## Liquidity Provision

### What is liquidity provision?
Liquidity providers supply funds to the insurance pool and earn rewards in return. These funds are used to pay out insurance claims.

### What are the risks?
- **Claim Risk**: If many claims occur, liquidity providers may lose principal
- **Smart Contract Risk**: Potential vulnerabilities in the code
- **Market Risk**: Value of rewards may fluctuate

### What returns can I expect?
Returns vary based on:
- Base APY (typically 5-15%)
- Duration bonuses (up to 50% extra for 1-year locks)
- Auto-compounding (10% bonus)
- Pool utilization rates

### Can I withdraw my liquidity anytime?
Yes, but early withdrawals may incur penalties and forfeit duration bonuses.

## Technical Questions

### What are gas fees?
Gas fees are small amounts of CELO paid to validators for processing transactions on the Celo blockchain.

### How can I reduce gas fees?
- Use gas optimization tools
- Transact during low-traffic periods
- Batch multiple operations together
- Use the Celo mobile app (MiniPay)

### Is my data private?
All transactions are public on the blockchain, but your personal identity is protected by your wallet address.

### What happens if the smart contract has a bug?
We conduct thorough audits and have emergency functions to pause the protocol and recover funds if necessary.
